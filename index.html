About Me

About Me

My name is Zachary Silliman. I graduated from Cornell University with a degree in computer science and a specialization in mathematics. I am currently employed at Framestore as an Immersive developer where I use Unreal Engine alongside a number of supporting tools to create big-screen, VR, AR, and pixel-streamed experiences. Two projects I have released with Framestore are One Vanderbilt's Unity/Transcenedence, as well as Cadillac's Art of You.


Through these and other projects I found an appreciation for bridging the worlds of the designer, artist and developer. Not to be confused with being at the intersection, as may be seen in a technical artist, building this bridge involves wielding technical knowledge to design and create tools that abstract away clunky details to accommodate designers' and artists' creative vision.

This construction project also manifests itself in my passion for game development and my current project: Ascend. This project has helped me define the intricate relationships between the designer, artist and developer. With just two of us working on the game, my role morphs between the software architect, gameplay programmer, tools programmer, level designer and game designer. I've come to understand and enjoy the intricacies in working alongside my friend who morphs himself similarly. To add more nuance, I live in the US and my friend lives in Taiwan (12 or 13 hours apart). We named ourselves Halfway Studios as an homage to the fact that we sit halfway across the world from one another.
